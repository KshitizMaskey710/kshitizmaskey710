## Agility before I had the word for it

Apparently, agility had been my secret sauce all along. I only discovered the word after stepping deeper into IT, yet the mindset had already appeared throughout my work.

## Client servicing was an early Agile classroom

Clients rarely arrive with perfectly fixed needs. Good servicing requires listening, making the work visible, testing understanding, and adapting as context changes. The vocabulary was different, but the rhythm of collaboration and feedback was familiar.

## Campaigns, events, and hospitality

Marketing and events taught me that plans matter, but response matters more. Guest needs change, campaign signals arrive, and coordination must continue without losing sight of value.

> Agility is not owned by one industry or framework. It is a way of learning and moving with people toward value.

## The lesson I carried into software delivery

Scrum gave structure and names to habits I already valued: short feedback loops, visible priorities, shared accountability, and adaptation. The framework became useful when it strengthened those habits instead of replacing judgment.
